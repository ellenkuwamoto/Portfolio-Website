Inspiration

Growing up with Japanese heritage, the table was the heart of our community—a space for connection, conversation, and shared experiences. Today, as eating becomes a task often done alone or on the go, I wanted to create a physical table that rekindles this sense of togetherness. It is inspired by East Asian dining conventions such as dim sum's lazy susans and the cozy intimacy of Japanese kotatsu tables. Also, drawing from the communal spirit of conversation pits—without the expense of digging a giant hole.

Target Audience

This table is crafted for the typical American nuclear family, with a compact size that fits comfortably in city apartments or suburban homes. Accessible and affordable, it’s designed to be sold through companies like IKEA, making meaningful connection at the table a possibility for everyone.

Functionality

This multi-functional table goes beyond eating—it's a hub for connection and collaboration. The outer ring holds plates, the middle ring spins like a lazy susan and locks in place, while the inner ring features an outlet, doubling as an analog record player or a hotplate. Designed for small homes, it incorporates storage within the chairs, keeping everything self-contained and efficient. With no screens in sight, the analog record player and communal hotplate encourage shared experiences, from cooking together to enjoying music.
