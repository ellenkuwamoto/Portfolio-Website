Inspiration

Inspired by traditional Japanese courtship, where women sat behind screen doors and men courted them without direct eye contact, I saw a striking parallel to modern connections through screens. In these interactions, men would gaze at the ornate screens while speaking, while the women could see each other. I wanted to display this dynamic of one-sided visibility that still exists across modern day screens.

Process

To make the piece, I laser-cut wood panels hinged together using red string to create a movable structure. The back layer features a scene of court ladies, screen-printed onto organza fabric, and sewn behind the wood. The top three layers are a landscape with each color printed on a separate layer of fabric. I then carefully cut them to fit each screen door and then attached them the top of each panel.

Concept

The project explores the layered structure of organza fabric, replicating the process of capturing a photo of a screen. The striation pattern mimics the appearance of modern LCD screens, while the layers represent the distance and obstruction in both ancient and modern relationships. The piece is intentionally difficult to photograph as the various components obstruct eachother from different angles and lighting conditions. The layers' visibility shifts depending on lighting, requiring proximity to reveal the hidden details. The final layer, sewn behind the wood in a muted color, creates a backlit silhouette effect.
